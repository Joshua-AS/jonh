Explanation:

The Fortran code reads the coefficients \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) of a quadratic equation, calculates the discriminant \(d\), and determines the nature of the roots based on \(d\).

  • a, b, c: Coefficients of the quadratic equation \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\)
  • d: Discriminant (\(b^2 - 4ac\))
  • root1, root2: Real roots of the quadratic equation if \(d > 0\)
  • rootu: Unique real root if \(d = 0\)
  • Complex roots are calculated if \(d < 0\)
